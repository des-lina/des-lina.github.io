About Me

I am a PhD student under the supervision of Peter Albers at the Mathematical Institute of Heidelberg University. I am a member of the Symplectic Geometry group within the research station Geometry + Dynamics.

My work is supported by the collaborative research center SFB CRC/TRR 191 Symplectic Structures in Geometry, Algebra and Dynamics and the research training group GRK 2229 Asymptotic Invariants and Limits of Groups and Spaces.

My CV

Research

I am currently exploring magnetic geodesic flows, primarily on HyperKähler manifolds. More broadly, my interests lie in symplectic and contact geometry and topology, as well as magnetic and Hamiltonian dynamical systems and billiard-type systems.
